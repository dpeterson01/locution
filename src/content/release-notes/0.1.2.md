## Locution 0.1.2

Cleaner cleanup, a simpler hotkey, and a faster first result.

## What's new

- **One dictation hotkey with a cleanup toggle** — hold to dictate and flip cleanup on or off, instead of juggling separate shortcuts.
- **See what cleanup did** — the overlay and history now show whether your text was cleaned, kept as a raw transcript, or fell back after a cleanup error.
- **The overlay shows the cleanup step** — short dictations used to sit on "Transcribing" while cleanup was still running. Now the overlay moves to "Processing" and lands on the finished result.
- **Faster first cleanup** — the cleanup model warms up while you speak, so the first result after a pause no longer stalls on a cold load.
- **Newer local models by default** — cleanup now uses `qwen3.5:2b` for short dictation and `gemma4:12b` for longer passages, with Metal-accelerated builds on Apple Silicon. Existing settings update on launch, and anything you customized is kept.
- **Rewritten cleanup instructions** — the built-in modes are tuned to fix speech without rewriting your voice or leaking instructions into the output.
- **Simpler modes** — a mode now carries just its prompt. Dictation length picks the cleanup model on its own, so there's no per-mode model to set.
- **Per-app rules by app name** — mapping an app to a mode no longer needs a hand-typed bundle ID. Type the app's name, like "Slack", and Locution finds it.
- **Offer to move into Applications** — when you launch from a disk image or Downloads, Locution offers to install itself into your Applications folder so auto-update and macOS permissions stay stable.
- **A focused language set** — the interface ships in English, Spanish, French, German, Simplified Chinese, Japanese, and Korean.
- **Steadier paste** — fixes a case where cleaned text could be dropped or a stale clipboard pasted in its place.

## Requirements

- macOS
- [Ollama](https://ollama.com) running locally for AI cleanup (`qwen3.5:2b` and `gemma4:12b`; Apple Silicon can pull the `-mlx` builds)
